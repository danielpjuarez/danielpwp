About Me

You may ask, "What does a bike connected to a shopping cart have to do with web development?"
Well, as you know, web development is all about solving problems. The problem can be figuring out how to most effectively advertise your business, or it can be finding a way to talk with your far-flung friends and family, but that root cause is always there.
I took this picture because I recognized, that, like McGyver, the crew of and team behind Apollo 13 (who had to figure out how to literally fit a square peg in a round hole using supplies available on the spacecraft), and the creator of this bike-cart (who clearly needed a find a way to carry a lot of stuff around):

  1. It is always possible to "improvise, adapt, and overcome"
  2. If it's stupid and it works, it's not stupid

My name is Daniel Juarez, and I'm an entry-level web developer and former Paramedic ready and eager to help your company solve problems, even if that means building a bike-cart. Come get to know me!

My Work

Peep

"Peep" is webapp that I created with my peer group, "Birbs" during my time attending the CNM Ingenuity Fullstack Web Development program. Using data from the Cornell Ornithology Lab eBirds API, it allows users to keep track of their bird sightings around New Mexico, and, after creating an account, save their favorite bird species.

Truck Check Form

When I volunteered with a fire department, we had issues with medical equipment on the Rescue unit (which is just an ambulance with better PR and lower mileage) not getting checked as it should. I created a easier-to-use, less cluttered checklist that made truck checks faster and more thorough. I also added hundreds of labels to the truck itself so new volunteers could quickly familiarize themselves with the equipment.
This was a simple, but quite functional, solution to a major problem.
