About Me

A bike connected to a shopping cart? You're probably thinking the same as I was when I took this picture, namely, "Hey, that's a good idea".
And it is. While this might have been intended as some avante-garde art-piece, or as a joke, the fact of the matter is that it's a perfectly functional bike trailer. Sure, you could go to the store and buy a fancy bike trailer purpose-built for your needs, or you could simply plop down a credit card and rent a car for a day. But what if there is no store close to you? Or you cannot afford to rent a car right now?
No, there's nothing wrong with conventional solutions. But what happens when you do not have the exact resources you need? Just like the person who created this ingenious "bike-cart", McGyver, and the crew of and team behind Apollo 13 (who had to figure out how to literally fit a square peg in a round hole with supplies available on the spacecraft), I have learned the importance of two things in my time working in IT, as an EMT, and in Web Development:

  1. It is always possible to "improvise, adapt, and overcome"
  2. If it's stupid and it works, it's not stupid
Whether it comes down to creating a pelvic binder out of a blanket and a strong stick, irrigating eyes with a bag of normal saline and big syringe from a Combitube, or helping your company build innovative web-apps and pages, I'm your guy. My name is Daniel Juarez, and I'm ready and able to help you solve problems.
